A Look into the Upsets

March Madness  ·  2008–2025  ·  NCAA Tournament

Every March, millions of brackets are filled out — and promptly destroyed. Upsets are the lifeblood of the NCAA Tournament, turning unknowns into legends and favorites into footnotes. This is the story of who upsets whom, when, and by how much.

When do upsets happen?

It's rare that any single year goes according to anyone's plan. That might be the reason why no one has ever gotten a perfect bracket. In 2022–2023 the tournament resulted in 17 upsets. Which matchups produce the most chaos?

All upsets are created differently. A 5-seed loss to a 12-seed is almost something to be expected. Though a 1-seed losing to a 16-seed? That is something that is a little more out of the ordinary. Which seeds are actually dangerous?

The win percentage isn't the only thing to measure. The PAKE score - denoted as Performance Above Kenpom Expectation - shows the seeds that are consistently punching above their own weight. A positive PAKE value indicates the seed is going to win more often than their strength-of-schedule would suggest. This is the hallmark of a potentially dangerous underdog.

Bar = historical win rate. Dot = PAKE score (green = overperforms, red = underperforms vs. expectation).
